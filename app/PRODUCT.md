# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Sandi Desktop is private household software for two known people: the user and
their spouse. In this document, `product` describes an application interface;
it does not imply a commercial product, a public audience, or prospective
customers.

Both users already know Sandi and her features. Design work should optimize for
their established habits and repeated daily use. Do not add tours, feature
discovery prompts, conversion flows, broad persona support, telemetry, sharing,
or multi-user administration unless a request introduces that need.

## Product Purpose

The desktop app gives the household's personalized agent a visible presence on
the computer and a direct place to talk with her. Sandi should feel like the
same living character whether she is wandering on the desktop, reacting to
work, or answering in chat.

Success means that Sandi feels present, specific to the household, pleasant to
live with, and dependable for everyday work. The app should not feel like an
interchangeable AI chat client wrapped around the agent.

## Brand Personality

Playful, warm, and a little uncanny. The reference is Tamagotchi-like ambient
life and attachment, without adding simulated needs or game mechanics unless
the household explicitly wants them. Sandi's character should appear through
her behavior, reactions, voice, and continuity across surfaces.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI chat applications whose identity stops at an avatar and accent
  color.
- Commercial SaaS conventions such as acquisition funnels, upgrade prompts,
  engagement tactics, analytics dashboards, and settings built for every
  possible user.
- Corporate-neutral interfaces that suppress Sandi's particular character in
  favor of scalable defaults.
- Novel controls that make familiar actions harder merely to add personality.

## Design Principles

1. **Design for the household.** Favor the two users' real habits over the
   expectations of strangers or hypothetical customers.
2. **Let Sandi carry the interface.** Her desktop behavior, chat presence, and
   operational state should feel continuous rather than like separate pet and
   application layers.
3. **Keep utility direct.** Conversation, attachments, history, errors, and
   other practical controls should remain predictable and precise.
4. **Reward repeated use.** Remove small recurring friction and prefer durable
   shortcuts over onboarding or explanatory chrome.
5. **Preserve specificity.** Household context and character are strengths;
   do not generalize them away to imitate a neutral product template.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

There is no formal conformance target. Preserve Sandi's visual identity and
character, while retaining low-cost fundamentals such as keyboard operability,
visible focus, readable text, and reduced-motion behavior. Known needs of the
two household users take priority over assumptions about a general audience.
